8 Most Beautiful Mosques in the World

Information - Published Friday, October 1st, 2021

Mosque Islam Tour Religion

Mosque is a place of worship for Muslims, because it is used as a place of worship for many people, the mosque is usually made large and magnificent.

Its splendor is seen from the architecture and interior details of the mosque that makes the mosque feel comfortable to worship.

In addition, some magnificent mosque buildings can also make it dubbed as the most beautiful mosque.

The Most Beautiful Mosque in the World

1. Haram Mosque, Saudi Arabia.

No one doubts the Grand Mosque as the largest and most famous mosque in the world. This mosque is the holiest place for Muslims around the world.

Almost every day, the mosque in the city of Mecca is filled with worshipers from all over the world, both those who perform Umrah, Hajj, and the surrounding community.

Having an area of ​​more than 40 thousand hectares, the mosque that surrounds the Kaabah can accommodate up to 4 million worshipers at one time.

Besides being spacious, the appearance of this mosque is also so majestic that it is called the most beautiful mosque in the world. The Grand Mosque has hundreds of entrances and towering minarets.

Inside the Grand Mosque there are many historical buildings, such as the Kaaba, Black Stone, Maqam Ibrahim, Zam-Zam wells, and others.

2. Prophet's Mosque, Saudi Arabia.

The Prophet's Mosque is the second largest and most famous mosque in the world, so it's no wonder it is included in the list of the most beautiful mosques. The mosque, which is located in the city of Medina, is the final resting place of the Prophet Muhammad.

This mosque was previously the house of the Prophet Muhammad SAW which he lived in after moving from Mecca to Medina.

The mosque building was actually built without a roof. However, for the convenience of the congregation, hundreds of large beautiful hydraulic umbrellas were placed in the courtyard area of ​​the Prophet's Mosque.

The umbrellas will be opened when the weather is hot. In winter, the umbrellas made in Germany are closed.

Not only makes the congregation more comfortable, this umbrella also enhances the appearance of the Prophet's Mosque and becomes a favorite photo spot for visitors.

3. Nasir al-Mulk Mosque, Iran.

Nicknamed the 'Pink Mosque', due to the light refraction inside the mosque which is purplish pink thanks to the stained glass in its windows and doors, the Nasir al-Mulk Mosque is without a doubt one of the most beautiful mosques in the world.

Equipped with Persian carpets that complete its beauty, this mosque located in the city of Shiraz is one of the masterpieces in the field of architecture that makes you feel like you are in another world when you enter it!

4. Sheikh Zayed Mosque, United Arab Emirates.

Sheikh Zayed Mosque is one of the largest mosques in the world which can accommodate up to 40 thousand worshipers at once! This mosque in Abu Dhabi also has 82 domes, more than 1,000 pillars, 24-carat crystal chandeliers from Swarovski, a beautiful pool, and the largest carpet in the world that will blow your mind.

5. Sultan Ahmet Mosque, Turkey.

Popularly known as the "Blue Mosque", the Sultan Ahmet Mosque, is a very prominent building in Istanbul due to its large size and beauty. The roofs are installed with 20,000 blue Iznik tiles that reflect light so that visitors can see the blue, green and turquoise glow of the mosque.

6. Hagia Sophia, Turkey.

Hagia Sophia, is one of the largest and most beautiful buildings in the entire world. Uniquely, this building was used as a cathedral for Christians, before being converted into a mosque by the Ottoman Empire. The building is considered the finest architectural relic of the Byzantine Empire, with its gigantic dome that can be seen from kilometers away.

Inside, the Hagia Sophia has marble pillars and gold mosaics that are sure to amaze visitors. Has been turned into a Museum since 1935, in recent years there has been talk of making the Hagia Sophia a mosque again like in the Ottoman era.

7. Al-Aqsa Mosque, Palestine.

Al-Aqsa Mosque is the third holiest place in Islam, after the Grand Mosque in Mecca and the Prophet's Mosque in Medina. This place is believed to be the location of the Prophet Muhammad praying in Jerusalem during Isra and Mi'raj, and is the initial mecca of Muslims before the revelation so that Muslims oriented to the Kaaba in the Grand Mosque down to the Prophet.

Although repeatedly damaged by natural disasters and tragic incidents, the Al-Aqsa Mosque has been rebuilt again and again with the architectural style of the early Islamic era that continues to be maintained. Still in the Al-Aqsa complex, stands the Dome of the Rock or Qubbat As-Sakhrah which is very iconic with its golden dome. Uniquely, people are often confused between Al-Aqsa and Qubbat As-Sakhrah.

8. Istiqlal Mosque, Indonesia.

The Istiqlal Mosque, the largest mosque in Southeast Asia, can accommodate up to 200,000 worshipers at a time, making it one of the largest mosques in the world. Not only is the size impressive, the architectural design of this mosque is also stunning, with the shape of the walls and pillars that characterize this mosque.

Uniquely, the mosque whose name means "Independence" in Arabic is the result of a design competition held in 1955. The architect was Frederich Silaban, and this mosque took 12 years to build until it was finally inaugurated in 1978.
